Life Expectancy in South East Asia

See how the life expectancy of countries in South East Asia has changed between the year 2000 and 2015.

The map on the left shows the life expectancy in years of countries in South East Asia from the year 2000 to 2015. Use the slider beneath the map to see how the life expectancy have improved throughout the years. Hover over each country to see more details about their population, GDP per capita and average BMI.

Life expectancy in South East Asia has shown significant improvement over the past few decades, reflecting advancements in healthcare, sanitation, and overall living conditions. South East Asia is a diverse region, consisting of 10 countries, each with its unique characteristics, but there are some common trends when it comes to life expectancy in the region.

Life expectancy varies widely between countries in South East Asia. For example, countries like Singapore and Brunei have life expectancies similar to developed Western nations, while others, such as Myanmar and Laos, have lower life expectancies. This discrepancy can largely be attributed to differences in healthcare infrastructure, access to education, and economic development.

Investment in healthcare infrastructure has been a key driver of increased life expectancy in the region. Countries like Thailand and Malaysia have developed robust healthcare systems that provide access to quality medical care. These systems have not only increased life expectancy but also attracted medical tourists from around the world.

Gross Domestic Product and Body Mass Index

The two graphs underneath show the GDP per capita and average BMI of South East Asian Countries from 2000 to 2015.

The GDP of each country, on average, has increased between 2000 and 2015. Singapore, Brunei and Malaysia's GDP are by far the highest among all South East Asian Countries.

The BMI of each country is on a steady increase throughout the years. This is possibly due to the increased wealth leading to better food quality and healthy eating.

These two factors contribute to South East Asia's increase in life expectancy between 2000 and 2015.

Health Statistics of South East Asian Countries

The parallel coordinate plot below shows the different health statistics of countries in South East Asia. These include:

Infant deaths - the number of infant deaths per 1000 people.

Measles - the number of reported measles cases per 1000 people

Polio - the percentage of 1-year-olds immunized against polio.

Schooling - the average number of years that people study.

Thinness - the prevalence of thinness among people 1 to 19 years old in percentage.

Under-five deaths - the number of deaths of people under 5 years old per 1000 people.

As health and wealth improves throughout the years, the number of schooling years and immunization against Polio increases, while infant deaths, measles, thinness and under-five deaths decreases. Scroll through the year slider below to see how these health statistics changes throughout the years.
